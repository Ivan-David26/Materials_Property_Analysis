# Bibliography and References

## Textbooks

1. **Ashby, M.F.** (2017). *Materials Selection in Mechanical Design*, 5th Edition.
   Butterworth-Heinemann, Oxford. ISBN: 978-0-08-100599-6.
   — Primary reference for merit index methodology and Ashby charts.

2. **Callister, W.D. & Rethwisch, D.G.** (2018). *Materials Science and Engineering:
   An Introduction*, 10th Edition. John Wiley & Sons, New York. ISBN: 978-1-119-40549-8.
   — Reference for materials science fundamentals, hardness-strength relationships,
   deformation mechanisms.

3. **Shackelford, J.F.** (2015). *Introduction to Materials Science for Engineers*,
   8th Edition. Pearson, London. ISBN: 978-0-13-385362-3.

4. **Davis, J.R. (Ed.)** (2004). *Tensile Testing*, 2nd Edition. ASM International.
   ISBN: 978-0-87170-806-9.

5. **Courtney, T.H.** (2005). *Mechanical Behavior of Materials*, 2nd Edition.
   Waveland Press. ISBN: 978-1-57766-425-3.

## Databases and Online Sources

11. **MatWeb LLC.** (2024). *MatWeb Material Property Data*.
    Available at: https://www.matweb.com [Accessed: 2024]
    — Room-temperature property data for metals, polymers, ceramics, and composites.

12. **National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST).** (2024).
    *NIST Materials Data Repository*. Available at: https://materialsdata.nist.gov
    — Cross-validation source for selected material properties.

13. **Materials Project.** (2024). Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory.
    Available at: https://next-gen.materialsproject.org
    — DFT-computed properties (used for context; not directly used in this dataset).

14. **Granta Design / Ansys.** (2024). *CES EduPack Material Database*.
    Cambridge University Engineering Department.
    — Material property ranges used for validation of compiled property ranges
    (CES Level 3 academic database; accessed via university license).
